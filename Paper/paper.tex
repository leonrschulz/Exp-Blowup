\begin{abstract}
  A well-known folklore result about propositional logic is that transforming a formula in disjunctive or conjunctive normal form can lead to an exponential blowup of the formula size.
  We formalize this result in the form of two theorems in Isabelle/HOL and discuss the challenges we encountered.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec_intro}

The metatheory of propositional logic has been studied for many decades and the subject of extensive formalization effort for many years.
However, to the best of our knowledge, very few quantitative results have been formalized.
One well-known quantitative folklore result is that transforming a formula in disjunctive or conjunctive normal form can lead to an exponential blowup of the formula size.

In this paper, we first briefly cover the necessary mathematical background (\cref{sec_background}) and then report on our Isabelle/HOL formalization of this exponential blowup.
We start by presenting a few preliminary definitions and lemmas (\cref{sec_preliminaries}).

We then prove the exponential blowup when converting a formula from conjunctive to disjunctive normal form (\cref{sec_CNF_to_DNF}).
We do this in two steps:
First, we prove the low-level \cref{thm_cnf_to_dnf_blowup} that assumes the existence of two equivalent formulas fulfilling some assumptions and that exhibits the exponential lower bound on the formula size.
Second, we use this \lcnamecref{thm_cnf_to_dnf_blowup} to prove in the desired \cref{thm_cnf_to_dnf_blowup_math} that such formulas always exist.

We analogously prove the same exponential blowup when converting a formula from disjunctive to conjunctive normal form (\cref{sec_DNF_to_CNF}).
We do this again with two steps:
First, the low-level \Cref{thm_dnf_to_cnf_blowup} assumes again the existence of two equivalent formulas fulfilling some assumptions and exhibits the exponential lower bound on the formula size;
the proof uses \cref{thm_cnf_to_dnf_blowup}.
Second, we use \cref{thm_dnf_to_cnf_blowup} to prove in the desired \cref{thm_dnf_to_cnf_blowup_math} that such formulas always exist.

Finally, we discus (\cref{sec_discussion}) some of the challenges we encountered when proving statements about the size of formulas.

\section{Background}
\label{sec_background}

Formulas of propositional logic are constructed from variables
(or \emph{atoms}) with the logical connectives negation $\neg$,
disjunction $\lor$, conjunction $\land$ and implication $\to$.

A \emph{literal} is a variable $x$ oder a negated variable
$\neg x$. The literals $x$ and $\neg x$ are \emph{complements}
of each other.
%The complement of a lieral $a$ is denoted by $\bar{a}$.

A formula $F$ is in \emph{negation normal form} (NNF), if $F$ contains
no implications, and negations in $F$ only occur immediately in front
of variables, i.e., $F$ is built from literals by conjunction and
disjunction only.

For a formula $F$ in NNF we define the dual formula $\bar F$ in NNF
by replacing every literal by its complement, and interchanging the
symbols $\land$ and $\lor$.
For every formula $F$ in NNF, $\bar{F}$ is equivalent to $\neg F$.

\begin{definition}
  We define the following classes of formulas:
\begin{itemize}
\item A \emph{term} is a conjunction $a_1 \lor \ldots \lor a_k$
  of literals.
\item A \emph{clause} is a disjunction $a_1 \lor \ldots \lor a_k$ von Literalen.
\item A formula in \emph{disjunctive normal form} (DNF) is a
  disjunction $T_1 \lor \ldots \lor T_m$ of terms.
\item A formula in \emph{conjunctive normal form} (CNF) is a
  conjunction $C_1 \land \ldots \land C_m$ of clauses.
\end{itemize}
\end{definition}

Note that every formula in DNF or CNF is also in NNF. For a formula
$F$ in CNF, the dual formula $\bar F$ is in DNF, and vice cersa.

Every formula can be transformed into an equivalent formula in DNF,
and in DNF, but the cost of this transformation is an exponential
increase in size in the worst case

\begin{theorem} \label{dnfblowup}
  For all sufficiently large $n\in\Nat$, there are formulas $F_n$ in
  CNF with $|F_n| = O(n)$, such that every equivalent formula $G_n$ in
  DNF is of size $|G_n| \geq n2^n$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The formulas $F_n$ are defined as
$F_n = \bigwedge_{i=1}^n ( x_{i,0} \lor x_{i,1} ) \land (\neg x_{i,0} \lor \neg x_{i,1})$.

If $G_n = \bigvee_{j\leq m} T_j$ is a formula in CNF equivalent to $F_n$, then the following two claims hold, which together prove the statement.

Claim 1. Each of the terms $T_j$ contains exactly one of the literals $x_{i,0}$ and $x_{i,1}$, for every $i\leq n$. In particular, every term $T_j$ is of size at least $n$.

Claim 2. For every vector
$\epsilon = (\epsilon_1 , \ldots ,\epsilon_n) \in \{ 0,1 \}^n$,
there is one of the terms $T_j$ in  $G_n$, which contains the literals
$x_{1,\epsilon_1}, \ldots , x_{n,\epsilon_n}$.
In particular, $G_n$ has at least $2^n$ many terms.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem} \label{cnfblowup}
  For all sufficiently large $n\in\Nat$, there are formulas $F'_n$ in
  DNF with $|F'_n| = O(n)$, such that every equivalent formula $G_n$
  in CNF is of size $|G_n| \geq n2^n$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
  Let $F'_n := {\bar F}_n$ for the formulas $F_n$ of Theorem~\ref{dnfblowup}.
  If there were formulas  $G_n \equiv F'_n$ in CNF with $G_n < n2^n$,
  then ${\bar G}_n \equiv F_n$ would be a formula in DNF with
  $|{\bar G}_n| = |G_n| < n2^n$, contradicting Theorem~\ref{dnfblowup}.
\end{proof}

\section{Preliminaries}
\label{sec_preliminaries}

A \emph{formula} is inductively defined as either an atom, falsehood ($\bot$), the negation ($\neg$) of a formula, or the disjunction ($\lor$), conjunction ($\land$) or implication ($\rightarrow$) of two formulas.
A \emph{literal} is a special case of a formula consisting of either a positive or negative atom, or a positive or negative $\bot$.
Basic operations on formulas and related lemmas are expressed for any atom type.
However, our main theorems instantiate the atom type with variables $x_{i,b}$, where $i$ is a natural number and $b$ is a Boolean value.
A formula is \emph{conjunctive} if it is either a literal or its first subformula is a literal and its second a conjunctive formula.
Analogously a formula is \emph{disjunctive} if it is either a literal or its first subformula is a literal and its second a disjunctive formula.

A \emph{valuation} is a function from atoms to Boolean values.
The \emph{evaluation} of a formula w.r.t.\ a valuation is the Boolean value we get after replacing all atoms with the specific truth values assigned by the valuation.
We say that a valuation $\alpha$ \emph{satisfies} a formula $\phi$, written $\alpha \models \phi$, if the formula evaluates to true w.r.t.\ the valuation.
A formula $\phi$ is
\emph{satisfiable} if there exists a valuation that satisfies it (i.e., $\existsOne{\alpha}{\alpha \models \phi}$) and
\emph{tautological} if it is satisfied by every valuation (i.e., $\forallOne{\alpha}{\alpha \models \phi}$).
Two formulas $\phi$ and $\psi$ are \emph{equivalent} if they have the same truth value for every valuation (i.e., $\forallOne{\alpha}{\alpha \models \phi \iff \alpha \models \psi}$).

\begin{definitionx}
  \label{def_set}
  The function $\tyAnnot{\cst{set}}{\tyList{\yA} \Rightarrow \tySet{\yA}}$ converts a list into a set.
\end{definitionx}

\begin{definitionx}
  \label{def_size}
  The function $\tyAnnot{\cstSize}{\tyFormula{\yA} \Rightarrow \tyNat}$ counts the number of atoms, $\bot$, negations, disjunctions, conjunctions, and implications in a formula.
  In the rest of this paper, we write $|\phi|$ for $\isaAp{\cstSize}{\phi}$.
\end{definitionx}

\begin{definitionx}
  \label{def_sizef}
  The function $\tyAnnot{\cstSizef}{\tyFormula{\yA} \Rightarrow \tyNat}$ counts the number of atoms, $\bot$, disjunctions, conjunctions, and implications in a formula;
  the negations are ignored.
\end{definitionx}

Because \cref{def_sizef} ignores negations, it always evaluates to a smaller number than \cref{def_size}.
\begin{lemma}
  \label{thm_sizef_le_size}
  Let $\phi$ be a formula.
  $\isaAp{\cstSizef}{\phi} \le \left|\phi\right|$.
\end{lemma}
%TODO: explain why we use sizef and not size.

\begin{definitionx}
  \label{def_BigOr_BigAnd}
  The functions
  $\tyAnnot{\cst{BigAnd'}}{\tyList{\tyFormula{\yA}} \Rightarrow \tyFormula{\yA}}$ and
  $\tyAnnot{\cst{BigOr'}}{\tyList{\tyFormula{\yA}} \Rightarrow \tyFormula{\yA}}$ respectively
  build the conjunction and disjunction of all formulas in a list:%
  \footnote{The infix operatior $\listCons{x}{xs}$ adds an element $x$ at the front of a list $xs$.}
  \begin{align*}
    \isaAp{\cst{BigAnd'}}{[]} =\; & \neg\bot &
    \isaAp{\cst{BigOr'}}{[]} =\; & \bot \\
    \isaAp{\cst{BigAnd'}}{[\phi]} =\; & \phi &
    \isaAp{\cst{BigOr'}}{[\phi]} =\; & \phi \\
    \isaAp{\cst{BigAnd'}}{(\listCons{\phi}{\phi{}s})} =\; & \phi \land \isaAp{\cst{BigAnd'}}{\phi{}s} &
    \isaAp{\cst{BigOr'}}{(\listCons{\phi}{\phi{}s})} =\; & \phi \lor \isaAp{\cst{BigOr'}}{\phi{}s}
  \end{align*}
  In the rest of this paper, we write
  $\bigwedge_{i = 0}^{\listLength{\phi{}s}} \listNth{\phi{}s}{i}$ for $\isaAp{\cst{BigAnd'}}{\phi{}s}$ and
  $\bigvee_{i = 0}^{\listLength{\phi{}s}} \listNth{\phi{}s}{i}$ for $\isaAp{\cst{BigOr'}}{\phi{}s}$.
\end{definitionx}

\begin{remark*}
  Our formalization always use nonempty list and the base cases of \cref{def_BigOr_BigAnd} could be be left unspecified.
  However, providing these base cases allows us to prove many lemmas for arbiraty lists without requiring a nonemptyness assumption that would need to be repeatedly discharged.
\end{remark*}

\begin{definitionx}
  \label{def_Fn}
  The function $\tyAnnot{\cst{Fn}}{\tyNat \Rightarrow \tyFormula{\yA}}$ creates a formula containing
  the conjunction of the exclusive disjunctions of each variable pair $x_{i,false}$ and $x_{i,true}$ with $i \in \{1,\ldots,n\}$:
  \begin{align*}
    \isaAp{\cst{Fn}}{0} =\; & \neg\bot &  \\
    \isaAp{\cst{Fn}}{(n+1)} =\; & ((x_{i,false} \lor x_{i,true}) \land (\neg x_{i,false} \lor \neg x_{i,true})) \land \isaAp{\cst{Fn}}{n}
  \end{align*}
  In the rest of this paper, we write $F_n$ for $\isaAp{\cst{Fn}}{n}$.
\end{definitionx}

\Cref{def_Fn} is chosen such that a formula $F_n$ can only be satisfied by valuations that assign different truth values to the variables $x_{i,false}$ and $x_{i,true}$.

\begin{definitionx}
  \label{def_dualize}
  %TODO: be consistent with abbreviations: Either always abbreviated or never
  The function $\tyAnnot{\cst{dualize}}{\tyFormula{\yA} \Rightarrow \tyFormula{\yA}}$ converts a formula to its dualized form by replacing each literal with its complementary:
  an atom $A$ becomes $\neg A$,
  falsehood $\bot$ becomes $\neg\bot$,
  a negation $\neg \phi$ becomes $\phi$,
  a disjunction $\phi \lor \psi$ becomes $\isaAp{\cst{dualize}}{\phi} \land \isaAp{\cst{dualize}}{\psi}$,
  a conjunction $\phi \land \psi$ becomes $\isaAp{\cst{dualize}}{\phi} \lor \isaAp{\cst{dualize}}{\psi}$, and
  an implication $\phi \rightarrow \psi$ becomes $\phi \land \isaAp{\cst{dualize}}{\psi}$.
\end{definitionx}

\begin{lemma}
  \label{thm_equiv_dualize}
  Let $\phi$ be a formula.
  The formula $\isaAp{\cst{dualize}}{\phi}$ is equivalent to $\neg\phi$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{lemma}
  \label{thm_is_cnf_Fn}
  Let $n$ be a natural number.
  The formula $F_n$ is in conjunctive normal form and
  the formula $\isaAp{\cst{dualize}}{F_n}$ is in disjunctive normal form.
\end{lemma}

\begin{lemma}
  \label{thm_sizef_Fn}
  Let $n$ be a natural number.
  $\isaAp{\cstSizef}{F_n} = 8 \cdot n + 1$ and
  $\left|F_n\right| = 10 \cdot n + 2$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{lemma}
  Let $\phi$ be a formula in negative normal form.
  $\isaAp{\cstSizef}{(\isaAp{\cst{dualize}}{\phi})} = \isaAp{\cstSizef}{\phi}$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{lemma}
	\label{thm_sizef_BigAnd_uncnf}
	Let $\phi$ be a formula in conjunctive normal form.
	$\isaAp{\cstSizef}{(\bigwedge_{i = 0}^{\listLength{\isaAp{\cst{uncnf}}{\phi}}} \listNth{(\isaAp{\cst{uncnf}}{\phi})}{i})} = \isaAp{\cstSizef}{\phi}$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{lemma}
  \label{thm_sizef_BigOr_undnf}
  Let $\phi$ be a formula in disjunctive normal form.
  $\isaAp{\cstSizef}{(\bigvee_{i = 0}^{\listLength{\isaAp{\cst{undnf}}{\phi}}} \listNth{(\isaAp{\cst{undnf}}{\phi})}{i})} = \isaAp{\cstSizef}{\phi}$.
\end{lemma}


\section{CNF to DNF (MDS)}
\label{sec_CNF_to_DNF}

Our formalization splits the paper proof of \cref{dnfblowup} in a \lcnamecref{thm_cnf_to_dnf_blowup}, which assumes the existence of formulas with specific shapes and properties, and a \lcnamecref{thm_cnf_to_dnf_blowup_math}, which proves that such formulas can always be obtained.
We start by proving a few basic properties of conjunctive formulas w.r.t.\ valuations.
We present the most interesting ones here and refer the reader to the formalization for more details.

We first prove a monotonicity property of satisfiability w.r.t. valuations.

\begin{lemma}
  \label{aux_lemma_prop4_step1}
  Let $\phi$ be a conjunctive formula.
  Let $\alpha$ and $\beta$ be valuations.
  If both valuations map every variable occuring in $\phi$ to the same Boolean value and
  if $\alpha$ satisfies $\phi$,
  then $\beta$ also satisfies $\phi$.
\end{lemma}

Next, we prove some sufficient conditions for a valuation to not satisfy a conjunctive formula.

\begin{lemma}
  \label{aux_lemma_prop4_step3}
  Let $\phi$ be a conjunctive formula.
  Let $\alpha$ be a valuation.
  If
  \begin{itemize}
    \item there exists a variable that occurs positively in $\phi$ and is mapped to false by $\alpha$ or
    \item there exists a variable that occurs negatively in $\phi$ and is mapped to true by $\alpha$,
  \end{itemize}
  then $\alpha$ does not satisfy $\phi$.
\end{lemma}

Next, we prove a lower bound of the size of $\bigvee_{i=0}^{\listLength{\phi{}s}} \listNth{\phi{}s}{i}$.

\begin{lemma}
  \label{thm_exp_size}
  Let $n > 0$ be a natural number.
  Let $\phi{}s$ be a list of formulas.
  If every $\phi \in \isaAp{\cst{set}}{\phi{}s}$ has $\isaAp{\cstSizef}{\phi} \ge m$ and
  if $\listLength{\phi{}s} \ge 2^n$,
  then $\isaAp{\cstSizef}{(\bigvee_{i=0}^{\listLength{\phi{}s}} \listNth{\phi{}s}{i})} \ge m \cdot 2^n$.
\end{lemma}

We can now prove that some specific formulas equivalent to $F_n$ (see \cref{def_Fn}) have a size exponential in $n$.

\begin{proposition}
  \label{thm_cnf_to_dnf_blowup}
  Let $n > 0$ be a natural number.
  Let $Ts$ be a list of formulas.
  Let $G = \bigvee_{i = 0}^{\listLength{Ts}} \listNth{Ts}{i}$.
  If all formulas in $Ts$ are conjunctive and satisfiable, and
  if $F_n$ is equivalent to $G$,
  then $\isaAp{\cstSizef}{G} \ge n \cdot 2^n$.
\end{proposition}
\begin{proof}
  We proceed with five steps:
  \textbf{(1)}~First, we show that every conjunctive formula $T \in \isaAp{\cst{set}}{Ts}$ contains for all $i \in \{1,\ldots,n\}$ either the variable $x_{i,0}$ or $x_{i,1}$ positively.
  We prove this step by negation, where we have to derive false for two cases:
  Either the variables $x_{i,0}$ and $x_{i,1}$ are both positively contained in $T$ or they are both not positively contained.
  In both cases, we proceed by defining a valuation that satisfies $G$ but not $F_n$.
  For instance, in the second case, we find a valuation that satisfies $T$ and change the assignment of the variables $x_{i,0}$ and $x_{i,1}$ to false.
  We then show using \cref{aux_lemma_prop4_step1,aux_lemma_prop4_step3} that this modified valuation satisfies $T$ (and thus $G$) but does not satisfy $F_n$.
  This means that $F_n$ is not equivalent to $G$, which contradicts the \lcnamecref{thm_cnf_to_dnf_blowup}'s assumption.
  \textbf{(2)}~From step 1, we derive that every $T \in \isaAp{\cst{set}}{Ts}$ has $\isaAp{\cstSizef}{T} \ge n$.
  \textbf{(3)}~Furthermore, we prove that for every Boolean list $\epsilon = [\epsilon_1,\ldots,\epsilon_n]$ there exists a $T \in \isaAp{\cst{set}}{Ts}$ that contains the corresponding variables $x_{1,\epsilon_1},\ldots,x_{n,\epsilon_n}$ positively.
  If this is not the case for a specific list $\epsilon$, we show using step 1 that every $T$ must contain one of the variables $x_{1,\neg\epsilon_1},\ldots,x_{n,\neg\epsilon_n}$ positively.
  We then define a valuation that maps all variables $x_{i,\epsilon_i}$ to true and $x_{i,\neg\epsilon_i}$ to false for $i \in \{1,\ldots,n\}$.
  We show using \cref{aux_lemma_prop4_step3} that this valuation satisfies $F_n$ but not $G$.
  This means that $F_n$ is not equivalent to $G$, which contradicts the \lcnamecref{thm_cnf_to_dnf_blowup}'s assumption.
  \textbf{(4)}~We then prove that the number of Boolean lists of length $n$ is $2^n \le \setCard{\isaAp{\cst{set}}{Ts}} \le \listLength{Ts}$ using steps 1 and 3.
  \textbf{(5)}~Finally, we show that $\isaAp{\cstSizef}{G} \ge n \cdot 2^n$ by combining the results from steps 2 and 4 to discharge the assumptions of \cref{thm_exp_size}.
\end{proof}

\Cref{thm_cnf_to_dnf_blowup} assumes the existence of two equivalent formulas $F_n$ (in conjunctive normal form) and $\bigvee_{i = 0}^{\listLength{Ts}} \listNth{Ts}{i}$ (in disjunctive normal form) and proves a lower bound on the size of the later.
We now prove that such equivalent formulas do exist and that the exponential blowup holds for all of them.

\begin{theorem}[Exponential Blowup from CNF to DNF]
  \label{thm_cnf_to_dnf_blowup_math}
  Let $n$ be a natural number.
  There exists a formula $\phi$ in conjunctive normal form with size $\left|\phi\right| = 10 \cdot n + 2$ such that every equivalent formula $\psi$ in disjunctive normal form has size $\left|\psi\right| \ge n \cdot 2^n$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
  We start by a case analysis on $n$.
  The interesting case is when $n > 0$.
  We define $\phi = F_n$ (see \cref{def_Fn});
  it is easy to show that $F_n$ is in conjunctive normal form (using \cref{thm_is_cnf_Fn}) and that $\left|\phi\right| = 10 \cdot n + 2$ (using \cref{thm_sizef_Fn}).
  To prove the last property, we assume that there is an equivalent formula $\psi$ in disjunctive normal form.
  We first obtain a list $Ts$ (by filtering $\isaAp{\cst{undnf}}{\psi}$) of all satisfiable terms in $\psi$ such that
  all formulas in $Ts$ are conjunctive and satisfiable,
  %TODO: think whether we could explain the trick with filter and undnf. Maybe extract to its own lemma?
  $\isaAp{\cstSizef}{\psi} \ge \isaAp{\cstSizef}{(\bigvee_{i = 0}^{\listLength{Ts}} \listNth{Ts}{i})}$ (using \cref{thm_sizef_BigOr_undnf}),
  and $\bigvee_{i = 0}^{\listLength{Ts}} \listNth{Ts}{i}$ is equivalent to $\psi$.
  From this last equivalence, we show that $\bigvee_{i = 0}^{\listLength{Ts}} \listNth{Ts}{i}$ is equivalent to $F_n$.
  This allows us to prove that $\isaAp{\cstSizef}{(\bigvee_{i = 0}^{\listLength{Ts}} \listNth{Ts}{i})} \ge n \cdot 2^n$ using \cref{thm_cnf_to_dnf_blowup}.
  We can prove that $\isaAp{\cstSizef}{\psi} \ge n \cdot 2^n$ by transitivity and, finally, that $\left|\psi\right| \ge n \cdot 2^n$ using \cref{thm_sizef_le_size}.
\end{proof}

We claim that \cref{thm_cnf_to_dnf_blowup_math} is a formalization of the paper proof of \cref{dnfblowup}.

\section{DNF to CNF (MDS)}
\label{sec_DNF_to_CNF}

\begin{lemma}
	\label{aux_lemma_cor5_step1}
	Let $\phi$ be a formula.
	If $\phi$ is in negation normal form,
	then $\isaAp{\cstSizef}{(\isaAp{\cst{dualize}}{\phi})} = \isaAp{\cstSizef}{\phi}$ holds.
\end{lemma}

\Cref{aux_lemma_cor5_step1} only holds because \cref{def_sizef} ignores negations.

\begin{lemma}
  \label{thm_dualized_disj_not_taut_impl_sat}
	Let $\phi$ be a formula.
	If $\phi$ is disjunctive and not tautological,
	then $\isaAp{\cst{dualize}}{\phi}$ is satisfiable.
\end{lemma}

\begin{lemma}
  \label{thm_dualized_conj_of_disjs_is_disj_of_conjs}
  Let $Cs$ be a list of formulas.
  If all formulas in $Cs$ are disjunctive and not tautological,
  then there exists a list $Ts$ such that all formulas in $Ts$ are conjunctive and satisfiable, and $\isaAp{\cst{dualize}}{(\bigwedge_{i = 0}^{\listLength{Cs}} \listNth{Cs}{i})} = \bigvee_{i = 0}^{\listLength{Ts}} \listNth{Ts}{i}$.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
  We prove this by structural induction over the list $Cs$ using \cref{thm_dualized_disj_not_taut_impl_sat}.
  The idea is that each element of $Cs$ gets dualized.
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}
  \label{thm_dnf_to_cnf_blowup}
  Let $n > 0$ be a natural number.
  Let $Cs$ be a list of formulas.
  Let $G = \bigwedge_{i = 0}^{\listLength{Cs}} \listNth{Cs}{i}$.
  If all formulas in $Cs$ are disjunctive and not tautological, and $\isaAp{\cst{dualize}}{F_n}$ is equivalent to $G$,
  then $\isaAp{\cstSizef}{G} \ge n \cdot 2^n$.
\end{proposition}
\begin{proof}
  We prove this by contradiction using \cref{thm_cnf_to_dnf_blowup}.
  We assume $\isaAp{\cstSizef}{G} < n \cdot 2^n$ and from that derive false in five steps.
  \textbf{(1)}~First, we know from \cref{aux_lemma_cor5_step1} that $\isaAp{\cstSizef}{(\isaAp{\cst{dualize}}{G})} = \isaAp{\cstSizef}{G}$, thus $\isaAp{\cstSizef}{(\isaAp{\cst{dualize}}{G})} < n \cdot 2^n$.
  \textbf{(2)}~Moreover, we show that $\isaAp{\cst{dualize}}{G}$ is equivalent to $F_n$ using, among others, \cref{thm_equiv_dualize}.
  \textbf{(3)}~Also, we prove using \cref{thm_dualized_conj_of_disjs_is_disj_of_conjs} that there exists a list $Ts$, where every formula contained in $Ts$ is both conjunctive and satisfiable, such that $\isaAp{\cst{dualize}}{G}$ is syntactically equal to $\bigvee_{i = 0}^{\listLength{Ts}} \listNth{Ts}{i}$.
  \textbf{(4)}~From steps 2 and 3, it follows that \cref{thm_cnf_to_dnf_blowup} holds for $F_n$ and $\isaAp{\cst{dualize}}{G}$.
  According to \cref{thm_cnf_to_dnf_blowup}, it therefore must be the case that $\isaAp{\cstSizef}{(\isaAp{\cst{dualize}}{G})} \ge n \cdot 2^n$.
  \textbf{(5)}~The result of step 1 contradicts the result of step 4, completing the proof.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Exponential Blowup from DNF to CNF]
  \label{thm_dnf_to_cnf_blowup_math}
  Let $n$ be a natural number.
  There exists a formula $\phi$ in disjunctive normal form with size $\left|\phi\right| = 10 \cdot n + 1$ such that every equivalent formula $\psi$ in conjunctive normal form has size $\left|\psi\right| \ge n \cdot 2^n$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
  The proof is analogous to the proof of \cref{thm_cnf_to_dnf_blowup_math}.
\end{proof}

\section{Discussion}
\label{sec_discussion}

Whereas most ideas of the paper proof can be directly used for the formalization, some intuitive mathematical proof steps required significant formalization effort.
This was most pronounced in the proof steps 2 and 4 of \cref{thm_cnf_to_dnf_blowup}, where we derived statements about the size of a formula from its properties.
To prove step 4, we use a general lemma (already included in Isabelle/HOL) about the cardinality of finite sets:

\begin{lemma}
  \label{card_inj_on_le}
  Let $X$ and $Y$ be sets. Let $f$ be a unary function.
  If $Y$ is finite, and
  if $\setImage{x}{f}{X} \subseteq Y$, and
  if $f$ is injective on $X$, and
  then the cardinality of $X$ is less than or equal to the cardinality of $Y$.
\end{lemma}

We use \cref{card_inj_on_le} to prove that the number of Boolean lists of length $n$ is less than or equal to the cardinality of $\isaAp{\cst{set}}{Ts}$.
We instantiate $X$ with the set of all Boolean lists of length $n$,
$Y$ with $\isaAp{\cst{set}}{Ts}$, and
$f$ with a function that takes a Boolean list and returns some conjunctive formula $T \in \isaAp{\cst{set}}{Ts}$ for which the predicate of step 3 holds.
Finding an appropriate function for $f$ and discharging \cref{card_inj_on_le}'s assumptions was surprisingly challenging:
The first assumption was the most intricate and its proof uses both steps 1 and 3,
the second assumption follows from step 3,
the third assumption easily holds.

To prove step 2, we introduced a new lemma, inspired by \cref{card_inj_on_le}, that uses a binary function that is right total w.r.t.\ the target set:
\begin{lemma}
	\label{card_le_card_if_mem_imp_ex_mem}
	Let $X$ be a set of elements of type $\yA$ and $Y$ be a set of elements of type $\yC$.
	Let $\tyAnnot{f}{\yA \Rightarrow \yB \Rightarrow \yC}$ be a binary function.
	If $X$ and $Y$ are finite sets, and
	if for all $x \in X$ there exists a $y$ such that $\isaApAp{f}{x}{y} \in Y$, and
	if the function $f$ is injective on all those pairs of $x$ and $y$,
	then the cardinality of $X$ is less than or equal to the cardinality of $Y$.
\end{lemma}

We use \cref{card_le_card_if_mem_imp_ex_mem} to prove that every $T \in \isaAp{\cst{set}}{Ts}$ contains at least $n$ atoms and thus that $\isaAp{\cstSizef}{T} \ge n$.
To do so, we instantiate $X$ with $\{1,\ldots,n\}$, $Y$ with the set of atoms of $T$, and the $f$ with the constructor for variables.
Finding the correct statement for \cref{card_le_card_if_mem_imp_ex_mem} was challenging but, once found, it was easy to discharge the assumptions using step 1.\\
